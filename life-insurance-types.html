What is Term Life Insurance?

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period (term) and is generally the most affordable option. It offers a death benefit to your beneficiaries if you pass away during the term period, but it does not build cash value like permanent life insurance policies.

Key Features:

  • Lower premiums compared to permanent insurance
  • Coverage for specific time periods (10, 20, 30 years)
  • No cash value component
  • Renewable and convertible options available
What is Whole Life Insurance?

Whole life insurance provides lifelong coverage with a cash value component that grows over time. It combines life insurance protection with a savings element, offering guaranteed premiums and death benefits.

Key Features:

  • Permanent coverage that lasts your entire life
  • Cash value that grows tax-deferred
  • Fixed premiums that never increase
  • Guaranteed death benefit
  • Ability to borrow against cash value

What is Universal Life Insurance?

Universal life insurance offers flexible premiums and death benefits with a cash value component. It provides more flexibility than whole life insurance while still offering permanent coverage.

Key Features:

  • Flexible premium payments
  • Adjustable death benefit
  • Cash value earns interest
  • Transparency in policy costs and earnings
  • Ability to skip premium payments using cash value
What is Variable Life Insurance?

Variable life insurance allows you to invest the cash value in various investment options such as stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. The cash value and death benefit can fluctuate based on the performance of your chosen investments.

Key Features:

  • Investment control over cash value
  • Potential for higher returns
  • Variable death benefit based on performance
  • Tax-deferred growth potential
  • Investment risk borne by policyholder
